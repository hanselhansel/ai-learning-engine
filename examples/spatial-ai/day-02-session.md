## C. Today's Lesson: History of Computer Vision & Robotics

### Why This Matters for You

You need to understand the history for two reasons: (1) in interviews, people test whether you understand *why* things are happening now and not 10 years ago, and (2) the history reveals which approaches failed and why, which helps you evaluate current companies.

### The Three Parallel Tracks

The key insight of Day 2: Computer Vision, Robotics, and Language AI evolved mostly independently for decades. They started converging around 2023-2024. That convergence is why we're here.

---

### Track 1: Computer Vision Timeline

| Year | Milestone | What Happened | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| 1966 | MIT Summer Vision Project | Marvin Minsky assigned undergrads to "solve vision" in one summer | Showed how badly we underestimated the problem |
| 1980s | Edge detection, Canny filter | Hand-crafted rules to find edges in images | "GOFAI" approach: humans write the rules |
| 1998 | LeNet-5 (Yann LeCun) | First successful CNN for handwritten digit recognition | Proved neural nets could do vision, but compute was lacking |
| 2006-2009 | **ImageNet** (Fei-Fei Li) | 14M+ images, 21K categories, hand-labeled | The dataset that changed everything. 3 years to build. Most peers thought it was a waste of time |
| 2010 | ImageNet challenge begins | First competition: 71.8% top-5 accuracy (SVM) | Established the benchmark |
| **2012** | **AlexNet** (Hinton, Krizhevsky) | CNN achieved 84.7% top-5 accuracy, 10+ points above runner-up | **THE inflection point.** Proved deep learning + big data + GPUs = breakthrough. Started the modern AI era |
| 2014 | GoogLeNet, VGGNet | Deeper networks, better accuracy | Showed "deeper = better" |
| 2015 | **ResNet** (He et al.) | 152-layer network, surpassed human accuracy on ImageNet | Solved the "vanishing gradient" problem |
| 2017 | ImageNet accuracy hits 97.3% | Machines surpass humans on image classification | Problem considered "solved" for classification |
| 2020 | **Vision Transformer (ViT)** | Applied transformer architecture (from NLP) to images | Transformers invade vision. Same architecture for text AND images |
| 2021 | **CLIP** (OpenAI) | Trained on 400M image-text pairs from the internet | Connected vision and language for the first time at scale |
| 2022 | **NeRF explosion** | Neural Radiance Fields for 3D scene reconstruction from photos | Vision moves from 2D to 3D |
| 2023 | **Gaussian Splatting** | Faster, real-time 3D reconstruction | Made 3D accessible and practical |
| 2024-2026 | **World Models** | Video prediction models (Sora), spatial AI (World Labs) | Vision + prediction + 3D + physics = world understanding |

**Key pattern:** Every major leap required three things simultaneously: (1) better algorithms, (2) more data, (3) more compute. This is the same pattern you see in LLMs.

### Track 2: Robotics Timeline

| Year | Milestone | What Happened | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| 1961 | Unimate | First industrial robot arm (GM assembly line) | Proved robots could do useful work, but only scripted |
| 1970s | Shakey the Robot (SRI) | First robot to reason about its actions | "Good Old-Fashioned AI" (GOFAI): hand-coded rules |
| 1980s-90s | Industrial robotics boom | Car factories automate with fixed-program arms | Robots succeed when environment is perfectly controlled |
| 1986 | Honda starts humanoid research | P1 prototype (precursor to ASIMO) | 20-year R&D program shows how hard walking is |
| **1990s** | **SLAM invented** | Simultaneous Localization and Mapping | Robots can map unknown environments while navigating. Breakthrough for autonomy |
| 2000 | ASIMO (Honda) | Walking humanoid, stair-climbing | Impressive demo, but purely scripted. No real AI |
| 2004-2005 | DARPA Grand Challenge | Autonomous vehicles in the desert. Stanford wins 2005 | Kickstarted the self-driving industry |
| 2013 | Boston Dynamics Atlas | Advanced humanoid, dynamic balance | Hardware milestone: robots that can fall and recover |
| **2015-2018** | **Deep RL for robotics** | OpenAI, DeepMind: robots learning in simulation via trial-and-error | Robots can now learn behaviors instead of being programmed |
| 2018 | OpenAI Dexterous Hand | Rubik's cube solving with a robot hand | Showed sim-to-real could work for complex manipulation |
| 2020 | MuJoCo goes free | Google DeepMind open-sources the physics simulator | Democratized robot simulation research |
| **2023** | **RT-2 (Google DeepMind)** | First large-scale Vision-Language-Action model | The moment vision, language, and action merged into one model |
| 2024 | **pi-0 (Physical Intelligence)** | 3B param foundation model, 7 robot types, 68 tasks | Proved "one model, many robots" is possible |
| 2024 | Figure 02 + OpenAI integration | Humanoid that can converse and act | LLMs controlling physical robots in real-time |
| 2025 | **GR00T N1 (NVIDIA)** | VLA model for humanoids, trained on simulation + real data | NVIDIA enters the robot brain market |
| 2025 | **Helix (Figure AI)** | First VLA controlling an entire humanoid upper body at high frequency | Full-body VLA control achieved |
| 2025 | pi-0.5 generalizes across homes | Works in new kitchens without retraining | Early evidence of robot generalization |
| 2026 | Commercial humanoid deployments | Figure at BMW, Agility at Toyota, BYD deploying 20,000 units | The "production" era begins |

**Key pattern:** Robotics progress was gated by (1) hardware cost, (2) data collection difficulty, and (3) the brittleness of hand-coded rules. Deep learning solved #3, simulation is solving #2, and scale is starting to solve #1.

### The Convergence Moment (2023-2026)

This is the most important part of the lesson. Three separate fields are colliding:

| What Converged | From Which Field | When | Example |
|---------------|-----------------|------|---------|
| Transformer architecture | NLP (2017) → Vision (2020) → Robotics (2023) | ViT, RT-2 |
| Pre-train + fine-tune paradigm | NLP (2018) → Robotics (2024) | pi-0, Octo |
| Scaling laws | NLP (2020) → Vision (2021) → Robotics (2024-25) | Skild Brain, GR00T |
| Multi-modal models | NLP+Vision (2021) → NLP+Vision+Action (2023) | CLIP → RT-2 → Helix |
| Simulation as data source | Games (2010s) → Robotics (2020s) | Isaac Sim, Cosmos |

**Your bridge:** You know how Claude went from text-only to multi-modal to agentic (tool use, code execution). Robots are on the same trajectory, just 2-3 years behind. Text → Vision → Action. That's the roadmap.

---

### Why NOW? (The Interview Answer)

If a VC or hiring manager asks "why is 2024-2026 the inflection point for spatial AI/robotics?", here are the five reasons:

1. **Transformers work for everything.** The same architecture that powers ChatGPT now powers robot control (VLAs). This means robotics can draft off $100B+ of NLP/vision research.

2. **Simulation can generate unlimited data.** NVIDIA Cosmos was trained on 20M hours of video. Isaac Sim can run 10,000+ parallel robot environments. The data bottleneck is cracking.

3. **Foundation models generalize.** pi-0.5 works across different kitchens without retraining. This was impossible 2 years ago.

4. **Hardware costs are dropping.** Unitree's G1 humanoid: $13,500. 1X's NEO pre-order: ~$20K. Tesla targets sub-$20K at scale.

5. **Capital is flooding in.** $10B+ deployed in the last 2 years across Physical Intelligence ($1.6B), World Labs ($1.23B), Figure ($2B+), Skild ($1.7B). Money accelerates everything.
